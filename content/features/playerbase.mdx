# Enhanced Playerbase <span style={{fontSize:'0.6em',verticalAlign:'middle',opacity:0.7}}>BETA</span>

The **Enhanced Playerbase** turns your local bans and warns into a shared, cross-community reputation
layer. Upload your records to the LACORE playerbase, share them with partner communities by a key, and
— if you choose — **enforce** partner bans automatically when a player connects.

<Callout type="info">
  The playerbase is **advisory by default**. Nothing auto-bans anyone until you deliberately turn on
  enforcement on *both* sides. Uploading and sharing only ever expose **hashed** identifiers.
</Callout>

## How it works

- **Records** — every ban and warn keeps its provenance (which community, which server, who issued it).
- **Players** — records attach to a shared *player*, matched across servers by **hashed identifiers**
  (SHA-256, no per-server salt) so the same person lines up everywhere without exposing raw identifiers.
- **Ghosts** — if you ban someone the playerbase has never seen, a **ghost** player is minted to hold
  the record. The next time that person connects to a participating server, the ghost is **claimed**
  (their real identifiers attach, the ghost flag drops).

## 1 · Upload your records

Local bans and warns live in `data/banlist.json` (warns carry `type="Warning"`). Share them from the
server console:

```
/lacore bans list     # count your local bans + warns
/lacore bans upload    # push them to the LACORE playerbase
```

Upload rides the write-only relay and your **license key** — no key, nothing is sent. Server-side the
records are hashed, deduped and attached to a shared player (a ghost if unknown).

<Callout type="info">
  Requires a [license key](/customer-portal/license-keys) (`setr lacore_license_key "…"`). Duplicate
  records (same local id from the same community, or same player + reason) are skipped automatically.
</Callout>

## 2 · Share with partner communities

In the dashboard under **Community → Players → Bans/Warns Database** you generate a **share key**
(`PBSHARE-XXXXX-XXXXX-XXXXX`). Hand it to a partner and they paste it to pull your records into their
list, tagged with your name. See [Players & sharing](/customer-portal/players) for the dashboard flow.

Sharing is **directional** (giving a partner your key lets *them* read *you*, not the reverse) and
**revocable** (revoke the key to cut every subscriber at once).

## 3 · Enforcement (opt-in, two-sided)

Enforcement makes a server actually **reject** a connecting player who carries an active ban in its
playerbase. It requires agreement from **both** sides:

<Steps>

### Owner marks the key "Enforce"

On the sharing side, flip your key's scope from **Read only** to **Enforce**. This permits partners to
auto-reject players *you* banned. Plain read grants stay advisory.

### Consuming server enables the convar

The server that wants to enforce sets:

```cfg
setr lacore_playerbase_enforce true
```

</Steps>

Only when the owner grants **Enforce** *and* the consuming server enables the convar does a shared ban
block a connect. A server always enforces **its own** bans when the convar is on; partner bans are
enforced only for partners who granted the Enforce scope.

<Callout type="warning">
  **Fail-safe by design.** The connect check never holds the deferral — it runs asynchronously and only
  drops a player on a *confirmed* ban. No license key, relay down, or any API error simply lets the
  connect proceed. A slow or offline playerbase can never stall your server's join flow.
</Callout>

## Privacy

Raw identifiers never appear in shared reads — matching is done entirely on SHA-256 hashes. Your own
raw identifiers are kept only so *you* can lift your *own* records. Communities you share with see the
player's name and ban reason, never their identifiers.
