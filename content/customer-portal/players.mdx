# Players & sharing <span style={{fontSize:'0.6em',verticalAlign:'middle',opacity:0.7}}>BETA</span>

**Dashboard → Community → Players** shows your community's shared **bans & warns** and the
**Bans/Warns Database** panel for cross-community sharing. This is the dashboard side of the
[Enhanced Playerbase](/features/playerbase) — for the server commands and the privacy model, see that
page.

## The records table

Every record you [uploaded](/features/playerbase#1--upload-your-records) from a server appears here,
attached to a shared player:

- A <strong>GHOST</strong> badge marks a player the playerbase minted from a record but hasn't met yet
  — it's claimed automatically when they next connect to a participating server.
- Records pulled from a partner's database carry that partner's name as a **source badge** and are
  **read-only** (you can't lift someone else's ban).
- For your **own** records you can **Lift** a ban (keeps it in history, stops counting it active) or
  **Remove** a warn.

Filter by All / Bans / Warns and search by name, identifier or reason.

## Bans/Warns Database (sharing)

The **🔑 Bans/Warns Database** panel at the top of the page is where cross-community sharing lives.

<Steps>

### Share your database

Click **Generate key** to mint your share key (`PBSHARE-XXXXX-XXXXX-XXXXX`). Anyone you hand it to can
**read** your bans & warns. Use **Copy** to grab it, **Revoke** to cut every subscriber at once.

### Choose the scope

Under **Partners may**, pick:

- **Read only** *(default)* — partners see your records, nothing auto-bans.
- **Enforce** — partners running enforcement may auto-reject players you banned.

### Subscribe to a partner

Paste a partner's key under **Subscribe to a database** and click **Add**. Their records flow into your
table, tagged with their name. Remove a subscription any time.

</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  **To actually auto-reject** subscribed bans on connect, the *consuming* server also sets
  `setr lacore_playerbase_enforce true`. It's off by default — enforcement always needs both the owner's
  **Enforce** scope and the server convar. See [Enforcement](/features/playerbase#3--enforcement-opt-in-two-sided).
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
  Sharing is directional and revocable, and only ever exposes **hashed** identifiers — never raw ones.
  Treat a share key like a password; revoke it if it leaks.
</Callout>

## Access

The Players page is gated by the **players** [team scope](/customer-portal/team), so you can let a
trusted staff member manage the shared playerbase without giving them the rest of your account.
